Horizon Scanning; Science vs Engineering Risk; & Odd Jobs in Venture - In Conversation with Lux Capital's Sam Arbesman⁠ & Danny Crichton

By Lawrence Lundy-Bryan

A little Friday treat for you all today. While I’m busy beavering away on the next couple of editions on surface-acoustic wave (SAW) chips and nano-mechanical chips, I wanted to share a recent interview I did with Lux Capital’s Sam Arbesman⁠ & Danny Crichton. It’s good old fashioned family fun, and Lux package it all up in video, audio and text form below. We cover:

  • How we do Horizon Scanning at Lunar Ventures (#stateofthefuture)

  • Science versus Engineering Risk (if it’s science risk, it’s not really for VC)

  • Odd Jobs in Venture Capital (what is head of research really, and why)

  • Quantitative versus Qualitative Venture Capital (the competitive advantage is qual if everyone is doing quant)

After listening back, Sam said something that caught my eye:

“Whenever there's a high dimensional search space, sometimes actually having a specific objective in mind is not the best way to get to there. Rather what you should do is just focus on interestingness or curiosity or novelty and then build new kind of stepping stones and then combine those in interesting ways.”

That really is an exceptional insight and very well put. That pretty much sums up the way we think about research at Lunar and what I am trying to achieve. We now have these building blocks in the State of the Future tracker, let’s call them the stepping stones. The task now is to combine those in interesting ways. As I’ve said before like combining surface acoustic-wave semiconductors with spatial audio. Or nano-mechanical computing with low-earth orbit satellites. This is where novel investment theses come from.

Danny also says:

“I keep calling it stochastic opportunity creation, but essentially bopping around in the right places where there are thought leaders on the edge of science, on the edge of technology”

This was in the context of the messiness of venture capital and why you can’t just turn up with metrics and algorithms and automate it all away, especially at seed, and especially especially in deep tech.

Anyway, dig in.
